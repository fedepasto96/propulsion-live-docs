Context: What We Need

CAMPAIGN SUMMARY
We are developing a custom SOC estimation algorithm (EKF/UKF-based) for the E4 battery pack, which uses silicon-anode pouch cells. Silicon anodes introduce significant voltage hysteresis that standard fuel gauges (e.g., TI Impedance Track) cannot handle without extensive characterization. We need a lab to run a full characterization campaign on our cells to extract the model parameters.

Tests Required

Test Protocol Temperatures Est. Duration (per temp)
1. OCV vs. SOC (GITT) C/20 pulses, rest until dV/dt < 0.5 mV/min, both charge and discharge directions 0C, 25C, 45C (minimum) 5-10 days
2. HPPC pulse tests 10s discharge pulse (1-3C) + 40s rest + 10s charge pulse (0.75C), at 10% SOC increments 0C, 25C, 45C 2-3 days
3. Full capacity vs. C-rate CC discharge at 0.2C, 0.5C, 1C, 2C to cutoff voltage 0C, 25C, 45C 1-2 days
4. Hysteresis minor loop Partial charge/discharge reversals at C/20 with OCV rest points, progressively smaller loops 25C (minimum) 5-7 days

Cell format: Silicon-anode pouch cell (exact spec TBD, likely NMC cathode, ~5 Ah)
Voltage range: 2.7V - 4.3V
Repeat per cell: 3 cells minimum (statistical repeatability)
Estimated total duration: 6-8 weeks
Target budget: 8,000 - 20,000 EUR

Part A — Universal Questions (Ask Every Lab)

These questions should be asked at every lab meeting. They cover technical capability, pricing, data delivery, and project management.

A1. Technical Capability

# Question Why It Matters Answer / Notes
A1.1 What cycler models do you use? What is the current/voltage range and sampling rate? HPPC requires fast sampling (>100 Hz ideally) for clean R0 extraction. Our in-house cycler is limited to 10 Hz, which is one reason we're exploring outsourcing.
A1.2 What temperature range can your chambers achieve? What is the temperature stability and uniformity (e.g., +/- 0.5C)? We need at least 0C, 25C, 45C. Stability matters for accurate OCV readings during long rests.
A1.3 How many test channels can you allocate to our campaign simultaneously? Running 3 cells in parallel per test cuts total campaign time by ~3x. If they can only run 1 channel, the campaign will take 4-6 months instead of 6-8 weeks.
A1.4 Have you characterized silicon-anode cells before? If so, for which customer / application? CRITICAL Silicon cells have unique behavior: long OCV equilibration times, large hysteresis, path-dependent voltage. A lab without Si-anode experience may underestimate rest times and deliver bad OCV data.
A1.5 Have you run GITT protocols with very long rest periods (2-6 hours per step)? How do you determine when OCV has stabilized? Silicon cells take much longer to reach equilibrium than graphite. The lab needs to use a dV/dt threshold, not a fixed rest time. If they say "we rest for 1 hour," that's a red flag.
A1.6 Have you run hysteresis minor loop characterization before? Can you describe the procedure you would use? KEY DIFFERENTIATOR This is the most specialized test. Many labs can do GITT and HPPC but have never done minor loop characterization. If they haven't, they can still do it from a written protocol, but it increases risk of errors.
A1.7 Can you run custom test scripts/protocols that we define, or do you only offer predefined test packages? We need precise control over pulse durations, rest conditions (dV/dt threshold), C-rates, and direction reversals. A lab that only runs fixed packages won't work.
A1.8 Do you have EIS (Electrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy) capability? If so, what frequency range? NICE TO HAVE EIS provides a more complete impedance characterization than HPPC alone. Not strictly required but would improve model accuracy. Useful for future aging studies.

A2. Data Delivery and IP

# Question Why It Matters Answer / Notes
A2.1 What data formats do you deliver? Can we get raw time-series data (timestamp, voltage, current, temperature) in CSV or similar? We need raw data for our own parameter extraction pipeline. A PDF report with summary charts is not sufficient.
A2.2 Do you provide processed / extracted parameters (R0, R1, C1, OCV-SOC curves) or only raw data? If they extract parameters, we should verify their methodology is compatible with our ECM (1-RC or 2-RC equivalent circuit with hysteresis).
A2.3 What is your IP and confidentiality policy? Do you require an NDA? Can you sign ours? Cell supplier data and our algorithm design are proprietary. We need confidence that test data and cell specs won't be shared.
A2.4 Can we access real-time test progress remotely (e.g., a web dashboard)? Or do we only see results at the end? A multi-week campaign is risky if we can't monitor it. If a cell fails or a test program has a bug, we want to know immediately rather than after 6 weeks.
A2.5 If we need to modify the test protocol mid-campaign (e.g., extend rest times after seeing initial results), is that possible? At what cost? Silicon cells are unpredictable. We may need to adjust GITT rest durations or add temperature points after seeing first results. Rigid labs that can't adapt are a risk.

A3. Pricing and Timeline

# Question Why It Matters Answer / Notes
A3.1 Can you provide a detailed cost breakdown for the full campaign? Specifically: equipment time, labor (setup/monitoring/analysis), consumables, reporting. Allows us to compare like-for-like across labs and identify where costs can be reduced.
A3.2 How is pricing structured: fixed price per campaign, time-and-materials, or per-channel-per-month? Fixed price transfers risk to the lab (if tests take longer). T&M gives us flexibility but no cost ceiling. We prefer fixed or capped T&M.
A3.3 What is the expected total duration from receiving cells to delivering final data? Please break down: queue time, test execution, data processing/reporting. Queue time can add 2-6 weeks on top of actual test time. We need the full picture.
A3.4 What is your current queue / lead time? When is the earliest we could start? Directly impacts project timeline.
A3.5 Is there a minimum engagement fee or setup cost? Some labs (especially large ones like Element) have minimum fees that make small campaigns uneconomical.
A3.6 What are your payment terms? Do you require payment upfront, milestones, or net-30/60? Cash flow planning.
A3.7 If we need to run a second characterization campaign later (different cell, or re-test after aging), would pricing be the same? Any repeat-customer discount? We may need multiple campaigns as cell suppliers change or as we iterate on the algorithm. Long-term relationship value.

A4. Project Management and Communication

# Question Why It Matters Answer / Notes
A4.1 Will we have a dedicated test engineer as a single point of contact? Multi-week campaigns need continuity. We don't want to re-explain the project every time we call.
A4.2 How do you handle cell failures during testing? Do you have spare channels / protocols to restart quickly? If one of our 3 cells dies mid-GITT, we need to know immediately and restart with a spare cell. A lab that waits until the end to tell us has wasted weeks.
A4.3 What safety measures do you have for testing lithium pouch cells? (fire suppression, gas detection, pressure relief, abuse-rated chambers) Silicon-anode cells can be less thermally stable than graphite. The lab should have proper safety infrastructure, especially for the 2C / 45C capacity tests.
A4.4 How do you handle cell shipping? Can we send cells via standard DG courier, or do you have a preferred logistics partner? Lithium cells are dangerous goods (Class 9). Shipping requires proper packaging, labeling, and documentation. The lab should be familiar with receiving DG shipments.
A4.5 Can you provide references from previous clients who ran similar characterization campaigns? Best way to verify quality and reliability claims.

A5. Bonus / Future Scope

# Question Why It Matters Answer / Notes
A5.1 Do you offer cycle aging / degradation testing? Could we extend this campaign into a long-term aging study? NICE TO HAVE Future need: understanding how model parameters drift with aging for SOH estimation.
A5.2 Can you run application-specific profiles (e.g., a drone flight power profile) for model validation? After extracting parameters, we'll want to validate the model against a realistic load. Running this at the lab saves us from doing it in-house.
A5.3 Do you offer any consulting / data analysis services alongside testing? (e.g., ECM parameter extraction, model fitting) If the lab can also extract our equivalent circuit model parameters, it saves us significant engineering time. But we need to verify their approach matches ours.

# Question Context Answer / Notes

B2.2 As a research institute, how do you handle industry contracts vs. funded research? Is there a minimum project size? How does your overhead / pricing structure work? Fraunhofer institutional overhead is typically ~100% on labor. Need to understand if a small characterization campaign is economically feasible for them or if we'd be forced into a larger research project scope.

B2.5 Have you worked with silicon-anode cells from Chinese suppliers before? (e.g., Amprius, EnPower, or similar) Our cells will likely come from Chinese silicon-anode suppliers. Direct experience with similar cells would significantly reduce risk.
