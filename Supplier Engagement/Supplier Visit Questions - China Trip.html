Part A — Universal Questions (Ask Every Supplier)

These questions should be asked at every supplier visit. They cover the critical topics identified in the export regulations analysis.

A1. Export Controls & Decision 58 Awareness

CONTEXT FOR MAARTEN
MOFCOM Decision 58 (Oct 2025) imposed export controls on Li-ion cells/packs with energy density ≥300 Wh/kg at cell level. Currently suspended until Nov 10, 2026 (Decision 70). E4 target is ~350 Wh/kg — above the threshold. All Chinese suppliers would be affected if controls are reinstated.
# Question Why It Matters Answer / Notes
A1.1 Are you aware of MOFCOM Decision 58 and its implications for exporting cells ≥300 Wh/kg? Gauges regulatory awareness and sophistication.
A1.2 What is your contingency plan if the export controls are reinstated after Nov 10, 2026? Shows whether they've thought about this risk proactively.
A1.3 Have you ever applied for a MOFCOM export license? For what products? What was the process and timeline? Indicates practical experience with the licensing pathway.
A1.4 Do you have any manufacturing operations or partnerships outside of China (e.g., SE Asia, Europe, US)? Non-China manufacturing would bypass Decision 58 entirely.
A1.5 Can you confirm the gravimetric energy density at cell level (Wh/kg) for cells you would supply for the E4 project? Is it above or below 300 Wh/kg? Determines whether Decision 58 applies to the specific cells being offered.

A2. Decoupled Cell + Assembly Model

CONTEXT FOR MAARTEN
We are evaluating a strategy where we source cells only from suppliers and have packs assembled by an independent assembler in the US or EU (e.g., Leclanché in Switzerland, Packet Digital or Re:Build in the US). This gives us flexibility to swap cell suppliers if export controls are reinstated.
# Question Why It Matters Answer / Notes
A2.1 Can you supply cells only (without pack assembly)? Is this something you do regularly? Core requirement for the decoupled strategy.
A2.2 What is the price difference between supplying cells only vs. complete battery packs? Quantifies the cost of decoupled assembly.
A2.3 What is the MOQ for cell-only orders? How does it compare to complete pack MOQ? Determines if small-volume cell orders are feasible.
A2.4 Can cells be shipped directly to a third-party assembler in the US or EU? Any restrictions or requirements? Logistics feasibility for decoupled model.
A2.5 Would you be willing to provide cell samples and datasheets to a third-party assembler for pack design qualification? Needed to qualify cells with the assembler.
A2.6 Do you offer any technical support (BMS integration guidance, thermal specs, welding parameters) to third-party assemblers using your cells? Reduces integration risk at the assembler.

A3. Cell Specifications & Certification

# Question Why It Matters Answer / Notes
A3.1 What is the maximum energy density at cell level (Wh/kg) you can produce today? What is your roadmap for the next 12–24 months? Confirms if they can meet the ~350 Wh/kg E4 target.
A3.2 What cell form factors are available (pouch, cylindrical, prismatic)? What do you recommend for a drone application? Form factor affects pack assembly approach.
A3.3 Do your cells have UN 38.3 certification updated for the 2025 test criteria (+85°C thermal test)? When does it expire? Mandatory for all shipments. Expired certs block shipments in 2026.
A3.4 Can you provide a full cell datasheet, UN 38.3 test summary, and MSDS/SDS? Required documentation for shipping and assembler qualification.
A3.5 What is the cycle life at the target energy density? What capacity retention do you guarantee at 500 / 1000 cycles? Critical for drone battery lifetime and cost-of-ownership.
A3.6 What C-rate (continuous and peak discharge) can the cells support? Drone applications require high power during flight.
A3.7 What is the cell weight and dimensions for the E4-candidate cell? Needed for pack design and weight budget.

A4. Shipping & Logistics

# Question Why It Matters Answer / Notes
A4.1 What is your preferred shipping mode (air vs. sea)? Can you handle both? Air has stricter SoC and labeling requirements.
A4.2 Can you ship cells at ≤30% State of Charge? Is this standard in your process? Mandatory for air shipment from Jan 1, 2026 (IATA DGR 67th).
A4.3 What is the lead time from order confirmation to delivery (for cells only and for complete packs)? Impacts project planning and stockpiling decisions.
A4.4 Do you have experience shipping to the United States and/or Europe? Any past issues with customs? Export experience reduces risk of shipment delays.

A5. Commercial Terms

# Question Why It Matters Answer / Notes
A5.1 What is the unit price for cells at the E4 target spec (~350 Wh/kg)? How does it scale with volume (100 / 1,000 / 10,000 units)? Core cost input for project budget.
A5.2 Is there an NRE (non-recurring engineering) cost for custom cell development? Affects upfront investment.
A5.3 What are your MOQs for initial orders and for ongoing production? Must be compatible with E4 project volumes.
A5.4 What is your annual production capacity? How much of it is available for new customers? Ensures they can actually serve the E4 project.
A5.5 What are your standard payment terms? Cash flow planning.
A5.6 Can you provide samples for qualification (both for Flyability testing and for a third-party assembler)? Cost and timeline? Needed to start the decoupled assembly qualification process.
