Cost Estimation Guideline – E3/E4 High-Capacity Battery and Charger

Purpose

This document provides suppliers and partners with instructions for performing cost estimates for the Elios 3 / E4 high-capacity battery pack and charger. Use it together with the technical material in this folder.

General Rules

The following rules apply to both the battery pack and charger cost estimations.

Volume / MOQ

  • Pricing volume: Quote at 1,000–1,500 units (MOQ) up to 6,000–7,000 units per year.
  • Batch splitting: Option to split production into batches of minimum 500 pcs for logistical purposes; to be decided later in the project.

Cost Scope – Include All of the Following

  • Tooling
  • NRE (Non-Recurring Engineering)
  • Certification costs
  • Logistics / shipping
  • Packaging

Quote Format

  • Currency: CHF (Swiss Francs)

Key Cost Estimation Rules – Battery

BMS (Battery Management System) and PCBA

  • Exclude from cost: BMS and PCBA (Printed Circuit Board Assembly) — design and actual PCBA are provided by us (PCBA assembled by our supplier).
  • Include in cost: Integration cost for the PCBA/BMS into the battery pack must be estimated.
  • PCB structure: One front PCB for the connector; one top PCB for the main components (actual BMS); one interface PCB in front to connect the cells to each other.

Battery Pack Design

  • Design to use: Our design (drawings and specifications provided in this folder). A 3D CAD file may be shared if needed; the PCBA will remain basic in the CAD as a placeholder for assembly description.
  • DFM (Design for Manufacturing): Suppliers shall modify our design only as needed for manufacturing (DFM).

Design ownership for the charger is to be determined. Depending on the final decision, suppliers may be asked to:

  • Manufacture to a design provided by the customer, or
  • Propose their own design based on the requirements above.

Suppliers should indicate their capability for both scenarios in their quotation.

Key Cost Estimation Rules – Charger

The following cost items must be considered and included where applicable:

Electronics / PCBA

  • Power supply design and components (AC/DC conversion, power factor correction)
  • Charging circuit and charge management IC
  • Protection circuitry (over-voltage, over-current, over-temperature, short-circuit)

Enclosure / Mechanical Housing

  • Charger enclosure (material, tooling, finishing)
  • Thermal management (heat sinks, ventilation, thermal pads)

Cables and Connectors

  • AC input cable (with regional plug variants if applicable)
  • DC output cable and connector to battery pack
  • Reference connector: legacy 12-pin (final type TBD)

Firmware / Software

  • Charging algorithm implementation (if applicable)
  • Communication with BMS (if applicable)
  • Any firmware development or licensing costs

Certification

  • Safety certification (e.g. IEC 62368-1, UL, CE)
  • EMC compliance
  • Country-specific regulatory approvals as required

Testing and QC

  • End-of-line testing
  • Reliability / burn-in testing
  • Quality control procedures

Packaging and Logistics

  • Charger packaging (retail or bulk)
  • Shipping and logistics
