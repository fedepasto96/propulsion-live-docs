E4 Battery Charger — Cost-Effective Asia & Global Supplier Scouting

Flyability • E4 Battery Project • Prepared Apr 2026
Companion to: Charger Supplier Selection Report — this addendum focuses on cost-effective alternatives

Strategic context. Initial outreach to US-domiciled, NDAA-compliant smart-charger suppliers (Packet Digital, GlobTek, Inventus Power, Titan Power, Custom Power) returned per-unit costs in the order of ~USD 1,500 for a 4-port unit, with multi-thousand-dollar NRE on top. In contrast, Delta Electronics (Taiwan) — the only Tier-1 Asian OEM already in the active list — quoted around USD 250 per unit for a comparable 4-port platform. Given that the E4 program is not bid-locked into US federal channels, this report broadens the scouting envelope to cost-effective charger OEMs across Asia (Taiwan, China, Japan, Vietnam, India) and adjacent global geographies (Germany, Czech Republic, Finland) that can credibly hit a Delta-level price target while still delivering BMS communication, multi-port operation, and IEC 62368-1 grade safety.

1. Strategy Shift — From NDAA Gate to Cost Gate

The original Charger Supplier Selection Report (Feb 2026) treated regulatory fit (US/EU) as a hard gate, with NDAA / FCC Covered List compliance functioning as a near-veto for any China-anchored supplier. The practical consequence was a shortlist dominated by US/EU custom-OEM houses whose smart-charger price points are 5–10× higher than what Asian Tier-1s can offer for a functionally equivalent unit.

Flyability’s primary commercial channels are not US federal procurement. The E4 charger ships to industrial inspection customers worldwide, alongside the drone. Treating NDAA as a soft preference rather than a hard gate unlocks a new tier of suppliers and substantially improves the cost curve. The Delta engagement — positive, professional, and around USD 250/unit at 4-port multi-bay scale — sets the cost benchmark this addendum is built around.

Scoping decisions for this addendum:

  • Hard requirements remain the technical CHG-labeled TRs (BMS comms, battery ID/handshake, active discharge, IEC 62368-1, OVP, status indication).
  • Soft preferences: CE (EU placement is mandatory), IATA-compliant transport mode, multi-port capability, and field upgradability.
  • Cost target: USD 200–500 per unit for a 4-port multi-bay smart charger (Delta benchmark ±).
  • Region focus: Taiwan, China, Vietnam, Japan, Korea, India for cost; Germany / Czech Republic / Northern Europe for hybrid “cost & quality” (mfg in Asia, design in EU).

2. Re-weighted Selection Criteria

Each new candidate is evaluated against six criteria, with cost competitiveness explicitly added as a discriminator.

  1. BMS Communication (TR-111/113/114) — Native SMBus / I2C / CAN / DroneCAN / UART support.
  2. Battery Identification & Handshake (TR-112/114) — Smart battery recognition, charge authorization.
  3. Active Discharge / Storage / Transport Mode (TR-117/118) — Bidirectional flow or discharge path to 30% / 60% SOC.
  4. IEC 62368-1 + CE (TR-107) — Reinforced isolation, EU placement readiness.
  5. Multi-Port / Drone-Native — Existing 4-port (or scalable) smart-charger product line and UAS-specific firmware experience.
  6. Cost Posture — Realistic ability to hit the USD 200–500/unit envelope at typical batch sizes (1k–10k/yr).

Ratings: Yes = strong fit on ≥4 criteria with confirming evidence; Maybe = partial fit / needs verification; No = fundamental gaps.

Mean Well is the world’s largest standard-product power supply brand and increasingly competitive on smart-charger / bidirectional platforms.   • TR-117/118 (Active discharge): Confirmed in principle — BIC-2200 is bidirectional and supports force-discharge via CAN; perfect physical match for “charge to 30% / 60% SOC” modes.

Risks

Not a turnkey drone smart charger — their platform is a programmable power stage. Need a system integrator (or Flyability firmware) to layer Battery-ID / handshake on top. Multi-port (4-bay) version needs to be a Flyability or 3rd-party mechanical/control-board build around 2–4 BIC modules.

Cost posture

Single BIC-2200-24CAN: ~USD 500–700 list, well under USD 300 OEM. A 4-bay smart charger built around a single BIC-2200 + 4-channel switching board can plausibly land in the USD 300–500/unit bracket at volume.
