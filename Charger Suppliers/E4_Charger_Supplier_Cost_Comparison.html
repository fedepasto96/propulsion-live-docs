7. Key Observations & Comparison Summary

Packet Digital

  • Complete 4-port solution designed from scratch for 6S smart battery ecosystem
  • Smart battery controls charge behavior (BMS handshake, current sensing, temp monitoring)
  • Dual input (AC wall adapter + 12V/24V automotive) — field-ready
  • High NRE ($152K–$203K) reflects full custom design + validation
  • Competitive unit cost at $1,276–$1,701 (500 units) for a 4-port system
  • Certification costs still pending — will increase total non-recurring
  • NDAA / FCC compliant (100% US manufacturing)
  • Already designing E4 battery pack — charger+pack synergy

EFT Mobility

  • Existing platform (NATO-Standard-Charger) — not a from-scratch design; proven UAV charger
  • Compact charger supports multi-battery charging; scaling to 4 ports requires only additional DC-Out cables (~CHF 44/port at 5K NDAA)
  • Built-in discharge capability (up to 50W) for transport/storage mode
  • Low NRE (€29.5K) for adapter customization only
  • 4-port NDAA kit: ~CHF 1,991 at 5K volume (vs PD ~CHF 1,310 at 500)
  • NDAA compliant option available (but ~2.5× the cost of LCC variant)
  • LCC 4-port kit: ~CHF 776 at 5K — cheaper than PD, but not NDAA compliant
  • IP55 rated — better defined ingress protection
  • Young company (<50 employees, founded 2019) backed by Quantum Systems

Overall Assessment

  • Different architectures: PD designs a bespoke 4-port unit; EFT adapts an existing scalable platform with inexpensive per-port add-ons
  • NRE vs. unit cost trade-off: PD has ~5–7× higher NRE ($152K–$203K vs €29.5K) but lower unit cost per 4-port kit
  • NDAA comparison: PD 4-port at 500 units (CHF 1,123–1,497) vs EFT NDAA 4-port at 5K (~CHF 1,991) — PD cheaper per unit but at much lower volume
  • LCC changes the equation: EFT LCC 4-port at 5K (~CHF 776) is cheaper than PD, with dramatically lower NRE (€29.5K vs ~$178K) — but not NDAA compliant
  • EFT’s Multicharger (€250K NRE + CHF 5,400–6,500/unit) is a separate integrated multi-port product
  • Pack+charger synergy: Packet Digital is also quoting the E4 battery pack, offering potential design alignment and negotiation leverage
  • Neither quote includes certification — this will be a significant additional cost for both paths
