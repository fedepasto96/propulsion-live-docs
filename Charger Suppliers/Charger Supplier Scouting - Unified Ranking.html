Charger Supplier Scouting — Unified Ranking

Updated: April 2026 | Project: E4 Battery | Owner: Federico Pastore

Purpose. This document merges the two existing charger scouting reports into a single, scored ranking:

  • Phase 1 — Charger Supplier Selection Report (Feb 2026): US/EU-focused, NDAA as hard gate.
  • Phase 2 — Cost-Effective Asia & Global Scouting (Apr 2026): cost-first lens, NDAA softened.

Suppliers from both phases are evaluated on a unified set of 6 metrics derived from the E4 Battery charger Technical Requirements (TR-82 to TR-102) and the Cost Estimation Guideline. Two suppliers (Chroma ATE = test equipment; Skycharge = docking station) are excluded as they are not charger OEMs.

Supplier Ranking — Composite Score

Scoring methodology. Each supplier is scored 1–10 on six criteria, weighted to reflect the E4 charger program priorities (6S / 26.1 V, BMS handshake, IATA transport mode, 1–10 k units/yr, USD 200–500 target):
  • Smart Charger Intelligence (SCI) 30% — BMS communication (TR-94), battery ID (TR-95), charge-authorization handshake (TR-97), real-time status reading (TR-96). The core “smart charger” differentiator.
  • Active Discharge & Modes (ADM) 20% — Bidirectional capability or discharge path to 30% SOC (TR-101, transport — IATA 2026 mandatory) and 60% SOC (TR-100, storage). A hard requirement.
  • Safety & Certification (SAF) 15% — IEC 62368-1 reinforced isolation (TR-90), OVP < 31.3 V (TR-93), CE/UL, thermal protection (TR-91). Table-stakes but discriminating on depth.
  • Cost Competitiveness (COST) 15% — Realistic ability to land a 4-port smart charger in the USD 200–500/unit bracket at 1–10 k/yr (Delta benchmark ±). Cost Estimation Guideline: quote at 1,000–7,000 units/yr.
  • Drone / UAS Pedigree (UAV) 10% — Track record with UAV chargers, multi-bay field units, or closely adjacent sectors (robotics, defense autonomous systems).
  • Industrial Scale & Stability (IND) 10% — Headcount, revenue, manufacturing footprint, and ability to sustain a multi-year program.
Formula: Score = SCI×0.30 + ADM×0.20 + SAF×0.15 + COST×0.15 + UAV×0.10 + IND×0.10 (max 10.0). Green ≥ 6.5   Amber 4.5–6.4   Red < 4.5

Tiering & next steps

Tier 1 — primary RFQ track (score ≥ 6.5)

  • Grepow / Tattu (CN) — 7.6 — drone-native; DroneCAN handshake; TA3200/TA300 references; aggressive OEM cost. China origin = NDAA soft risk.
  • Packet Digital (US) — 7.5 — only supplier with confirmed BMS handshake + auto-discharge on mil-grade UAS. Premium US pricing (~$1,500). NDAA-clean.
  • Delta Electronics (TW) — 7.1 — benchmark cost ($250/unit); Tier-1 certs; already engaged. Active discharge not yet confirmed.
  • Foxtech (CN) — 6.7 — smart charger + Diamond BMS ecosystem; strong OEM pricing. China origin.
  • Mean Well (TW) — 6.7 — BIC-2200 bidirectional CAN; strongest discharge hardware. Power stage only — handshake layer external.
  • Phihong (TW) — 6.6 — Tier-1 TW OEM; CAN robot charger line; drones named; Vietnam mfg option.
  • Tulip Tech (NL) — 6.5 — DroneCAN ecosystem; 100+ drone partners. Proprietary lock-in risk if BMS doesn’t match E4 BQ41Z90.

Total: 34 suppliers (7 Tier-1, 14 Tier-2, 13 Tier-3).
Regional split: 7 Taiwan, 7 China, 7 US/UK, 10 EU, 2 Global/India, 1 Finland/hybrid.
Key insight: the top 7 span three very different supplier archetypes: drone-native Chinese OEMs (Grepow, Foxtech) offer the best price + UAV DNA combination; Tier-1 Taiwanese industrials (Delta, Phihong, Mean Well) offer the best cert + cost balance; US/EU specialists (Packet Digital, Tulip, EFT) offer the best handshake maturity but at premium cost. A dual-source strategy combining one Asian Tier-1 (cost) with one US/EU specialist (NDAA / reference design) covers the full requirements envelope.
