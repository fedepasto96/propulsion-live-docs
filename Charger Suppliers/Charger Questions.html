CHARGER SUMMARY
The E4 charger is a smart charger, not a simple CC/CV power supply. It must communicate with the BMS, authenticate the battery, and actively discharge to 30% SOC for IATA air transport. The BMS is Flyability-designed (TI BQ41z90 gauge IC). Communication protocol is TBD (SMBus, I2C, UART, or CAN).
KEY DIFFERENTIATORS vs. STANDARD CHARGER
  • Active discharge (bidirectional power stage) — mandatory for IATA 2026
  • BMS handshake — charger charges only after BMS authorization
  • Battery authentication — reject unknown/counterfeit batteries
Most off-the-shelf Li-ion chargers cannot do these. This is the primary filter.

1. Core Charger Capability

CRITICAL 1.1 Can your platform support active discharge (drawing energy from the battery back through the charger)? We need to discharge packs to 30% SOC for air transport and 60% for long-term storage.

This is a hard filter. Most chargers are unidirectional. We need a bidirectional power stage or a dedicated discharge path.

CRITICAL 1.2 Can the charger implement a charge authorization handshake via digital communication with the BMS? (Charger requests permission → BMS checks conditions → BMS sends ACK → charger starts.)

The charger must not start charging until the BMS explicitly authorizes it. This protects against cell damage if the BMS detects a fault.

CRITICAL 1.3 Which communication protocols does your platform support natively? (SMBus, I2C, CAN, UART, other?) Can you add a protocol that isn't currently supported?

Our BMS protocol is TBD. We need a supplier that can adapt to whichever protocol we choose.

CRITICAL 1.4 Can the charger read a battery unique ID from the BMS and refuse to charge if the ID is unknown or fails authentication?

IMPORTANT 1.5 What real-time telemetry can the charger read from the BMS during charging? (Individual cell voltages, pack temperature, SOC, fault flags?) How does it react to a fault flag mid-charge?

IMPORTANT 1.6 Can the charger firmware be updated in the field? (USB, BLE, wired interface?) We expect to iterate on charging profiles post-launch.

2. Electrical & Safety

CRITICAL 2.1 Is the charger designed to meet IEC 62368-1 (reinforced isolation between mains and output)? Do you handle the certification, or does the customer?

IMPORTANT 2.2 What is the maximum output voltage under any fault condition? Our pack requires OVP < 31.3 V (hard limit set by cell chemistry and 6S config).

IMPORTANT 2.3 What protections are built into the charger hardware? (OVP, OCP, OTP, short-circuit, reverse polarity, ground fault?)

2.4 What is the input voltage range? We need worldwide AC compatibility (100–240 V, 50/60 Hz).

Notes:

3. Production & Scalability

CRITICAL 3.1 Can you deliver 6,000–7,000 chargers/year at steady state? What is your current available capacity?

IMPORTANT 3.2 What is the typical timeline from project kick-off to first prototypes, and from first article to production?

IMPORTANT 3.3 Where is the charger physically assembled? (Country, site.) Is there a US assembly option?

US assembly preferred for FCC/NDAA, but not a hard requirement for chargers as it is for packs.

3.4 What is your MOQ for a first prototype run? And for production batches?

Notes:

4. Commercial

CRITICAL 4.1 What is the estimated NRE for a custom charger with these features? What does it cover? (HW design, FW development, certification, tooling?)

IMPORTANT 4.2 Can you provide a rough unit cost estimate at 6,000–7,000 units/year?

IMPORTANT 4.3 Who owns the charger design IP at the end of the program? (Flyability, supplier, or shared?)

4.4 Are you open to signing an NDA before we share detailed BMS specs and charging profiles?

Supplier-Specific Questions

Tailored questions based on initial research. Ask these on top of the universal questions above.

Packet Digital

Fargo, ND — Already builds smart drone chargers for Lockheed Indago, Anduril Ghost

PD1. You already make chargers with BMS handshake and auto-discharge for mil-grade drones. Can you share what discharge power and method you use? (Resistive bleed vs. bidirectional converter?)

PD2. If we bundle pack + charger, is there a meaningful cost advantage vs. sourcing separately?

PD3. Your fleet management software includes charger telemetry. Is that available as a standalone module, or only with Packet Digital packs?

Notes:

GlobTek

Northvale, NJ — TI-based charging platforms, SMBus native, IEC 62368-1

GT1. Your TI-based platform has SMBus. If we choose a non-SMBus protocol (e.g., CAN or UART), what is the effort to add it?

GT2. Active discharge is not a standard feature. Is it feasible within your existing HW architecture, or would it require a new power stage design?

GT3. Your manufacturing is split between NJ and Suzhou. Where would our 6–7K/yr volume be assembled?
