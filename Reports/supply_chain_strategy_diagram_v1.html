E4 Battery — Supply Chain Strategy

Dual-scenario architecture to serve US (50%) and global (50%) markets under current regulatory constraints • Feb 2026

SCENARIO A US Cells (ENPOWER / Amprius) — Preferred

Single US cell source feeds both US and EU assembly lines. Requires cells ≤ 350 Wh/kg (at cell level) to maintain EAR99 status and avoid US re-export controls on global sales from Switzerland.

SCENARIO B BEI Cells (Korea) — Fallback / Regulation-Proof

BEI (Korea, US-owned) is the only cell source with zero regulatory risk across all regimes. Preferred if ENPOWER cannot confirm ≤ 350 Wh/kg or is commercially unviable. Trade-off: higher cell cost; Korean origin does not contribute to FCC Buy American content for US market.

Key Verification Points

1. Cell manufacturers AND assemblers must be NDAA-compliant

For federal/DoD sales, neither the cell origin nor the assembly location can involve a "foreign entity of concern" (Chinese companies). Both ENPOWER/Amprius and BEI satisfy this. US assemblers (Packet Digital, Re:Build) satisfy this. EU assemblers (Leclanché, Tulip) are not entities of concern but don't contribute US content.

2. Cell origin should be a NATO / Wassenaar-aligned country

US, Korea, Japan, EU member states are all NATO or Wassenaar members. This ensures the cell source is not subject to entity-of-concern restrictions and aligns with allied-nation supply chain expectations for defense-adjacent products.

3. Chinese cells: conditional only

Chinese cells (Spard, Grepow, CBAK) can be used as a cost-optimized alternative for the EU assembly line only while MOFCOM Decision 58 remains suspended (exp. Nov 10, 2026). If reinstated, cells ≥ 300 Wh/kg cannot leave China without a discretionary export license. Chinese cells also block FCC and NDAA compliance for US market.

4. Both scenarios ensure redundancy and global coverage

Same pack design, same BMS, same connectors — only the cell source and assembly location differ. If one supply chain is disrupted, the other continues. Both scenarios allow Flyability to sell in all countries (US + global) without regulatory blockers.

⚡ Decision Gate: Which Scenario?

Step 1: Ask ENPOWER to confirm cell-level energy density is ≤ 350 Wh/kg + pricing at 250 / 2,000 / 6,000 unit volumes.
Step 2: If confirmed → Scenario A is primary. BEI remains qualified as backup.
Step 3: If ENPOWER > 350 Wh/kg or unviable → Scenario B becomes primary. Accept cost premium for regulatory certainty.
Step 4: Regardless of A or B — monitor Decision 58 expiry (Nov 2026) and Flyability's drone-level FCC pathway decision.
