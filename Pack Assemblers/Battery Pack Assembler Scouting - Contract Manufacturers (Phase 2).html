E4 Battery Pack Assembler Scouting — Contract Manufacturers / EMS (Phase 2)

Updated: April 2026 | Project: E4 Battery | Owner: Federico Pastore

Purpose. This is a complementary list to the existing Battery Pack Assembler Scouting (drone-native pack houses such as Tulip, Packet Digital, Re:Build, Leclanché, etc.). The candidates below are large contract manufacturers (CM) / Electronic Manufacturing Services (EMS) providers that take a "build-to-print" approach — i.e. companies similar to VDL, which Tulip itself uses as its physical assembler. The strategic intent is to bypass intermediaries (Tulip, BMZ, etc.) and contract directly with the underlying factory, capturing their scale, automation, low‑cost regional footprints (Eastern Europe / North Africa / Mexico) and clean‑room/laser‑welding infrastructure, while still owning our own design and BMS.

Profile we are targeting: Tier‑1/2 EMS or industrial CM with (1) laser tab welding or ultrasonic bonding (mandatory for pouch cells), (2) humidity / dry‑room or Class 7‑8 cleanroom for cell handling, (3) traceability software, (4) ideally ISO 13485 (medical) and/or IATF 16949 (automotive) which already imply lithium‑safe processes, and (5) capability for low‑to‑medium volumes (Flyability target: 6–7 k packs/yr, 1 kg, 6–7 pouch cells).

Add-on logic. Companies already present in the existing scouting list (BMZ Group, VARTA, Tulip, Leclanché, Enix, etc.) are intentionally not repeated here.

Supplier Ranking — Composite Score

Scoring methodology. Each company is scored 1–10 on five criteria, weighted to reflect Flyability's priorities for a 1 kg / 6–7 pouch cell / 6–7k units/yr build-to-print drone battery program:
  • Battery Pack Flexibility (BPF) 35% — how closely the company's existing setup matches our product: pouch cell tab welding, small-format (1 kg), 6–7k units/yr, and willingness to engage on small programs.
  • Assembly Process Proficiency (APP) 35% — laser/ultrasonic tab welding capability, dry-room/humidity control, traceability, and certifications (ISO 13485 / IATF 16949 / AS9100).
  • Production Volume (VOL) 10% — annual battery packs or equivalent units assembled, as proxy for operational maturity.
  • Employees (EMP) 10% — headcount as indicator of industrial stability and capacity buffer.
  • Annual Revenue (REV) 10% — financial scale and long-term viability as a partner.

1. VDL Groep — VDL ETG + VDL Nedcar Mobility Innovation Centre EU/NL

Why on the list. VDL is the explicit reference for the "no-design, assembly-only / build-to-print" model the user asked us to find. VDL ETG Projects is a multi-site mechatronic / industrial CM that builds for ASML, Thermo Fisher and other complex OEMs (low-volume, high-mix — exactly Flyability's profile). VDL Nedcar + the Mobility Innovation Centre (MIC) in Born run a 2,000 m² battery demo line at ~40k packs/yr (currently using ~10% of available capacity), with a planned 30,000 m² expansion described as the largest battery production facility in the Netherlands. VDL just secured a long-term contract to assemble ~125k battery modules for BMW Group's after-sales business through 2035. ETG is the natural entry door for a 1 kg / 6–7 cell pouch program.

What to verify on a discovery call: minimum order quantity / minimum revenue threshold, willingness to do small-format pouch packs, dry-room and laser-welding availability for non-automotive sizes.

2. Webasto — Battery Solutions OEM EU/DE

Why on the list. Of all the candidates Webasto is the only one that explicitly publishes a "build-to-print" battery offering. Production lines in Schierling (Regensburg) — €11M investment for a multi-product line that already builds packs for a European bus maker — and Hengersberg (Deggendorf) — €18M prototype + validation centre for aluminium battery housings. 3. Zollner Elektronik AG EU/DE + HU/RO/TN

Why on the list. Zollner is one of the cleanest VDL analogues in continental Europe: Top-20 global EMS, 26 sites worldwide, more than 10,000 employees in Europe, and the full process toolkit relevant to pouch cells — SMT/THT, laser welding, ultrasonic and thick/thin wire bonding, conformal coating, cleanroom production, X-ray, AOI/SPI, functional test, full traceability. What to verify on a discovery call: which specific Zollner sites have an established lithium pack line / dry-room (Hungary or Romania most likely), and whether they take small-format drone programs.

4. Triathlon Batterien GmbH (Triathlon Holding) EU/DE

Why on the list. Triathlon's plant in Glauchau, Germany was, at its launch, described as "Europe's most up-to-date battery assembly plant". In 2025 Triathlon Holding completed the acquisition of Voltabox's VoltaMobil high-voltage battery activities, consolidating that capability in-house.
