Battery Pack Assembly — Outreach Email Template (Phase 2)

Prepared: April 30, 2026  |  Owner: Federico Pastore  |  Companion file: Battery Pack Assembler Scouting — Contract Manufacturers (Phase 2).html

Purpose. Single generalised cold-outreach template for the 21 large contract manufacturers / EMS providers identified in the Phase 2 scouting list (VDL-style build-to-print partners, distinct from the Phase 1 drone-native pack houses). The template is intentionally short — EMS sales teams receive hundreds of inbound leads per quarter, and a concise, well-routed email is more likely to reach the right business-development owner than a long capability brief.

How to use. Copy the body below, replace the [YELLOW] placeholders, optionally inject a [GREEN] per-company hook, and send. Keep paragraphs as-is unless adapting for a Tier-3 / scale-mismatched target (see softening note further down).

Generalised template

To: [contact email or web form — see contact directory in Phase 2 scouting file]
Cc: [optional: named account / BD manager found via LinkedIn]
Subject: Build-to-print partner search — drone Li-ion pouch packs (Flyability)

Dear [Company] Team,

I am reaching out from Flyability SA (Switzerland), the manufacturer of the Elios family of indoor inspection drones used worldwide in the energy, mining, oil & gas, and many other sectors.

We are scouting build-to-print contract manufacturing partners for our next-generation onboard battery, with these key parameters:

  • Format: ~1 kg pack, 6–7 Li-ion pouch cells, custom mechanics
  • Volume: 6–7 k packs / year, ramping over 2028–2029
  • Scope split: Flyability owns the full design (mechanics, BMS, firmware, cell selection); we are looking for a partner to industrialise and serially assemble the pack to print.

Could we schedule a 30-minute discovery call in the next 2–3 weeks to discuss feasibility, MOQ expectations, indicative pricing logic, and a potential NDA / RFQ path?

If you can route this to your business-development team for the Battery / Robotics segment, that would be much appreciated. Happy to share detailed specifications and technical requirements under NDA on request.

Best regards,
Federico Pastore
Lead Battery Engineer, Flyability SA, Paudex

Adaptation notes

Greeting line

  • Default: Dear [Company] Team, — safe for all generic info@ / web-form sends.
  • If a named contact is known (Videoton: Horáth; VDL ETG: infoprojects@; NOTE: Mackinovski; Scanfil: Smeds; Triathlon: format firstname.lastname@): Dear Mr./Ms. [Last Name], always outperforms the generic team greeting.

Subject line variants

  • Default: "Build-to-print partner search — drone Li-ion pouch packs (Flyability)"
  • For US/ITAR targets (Sanmina, Benchmark, Celestica, Flex, Jabil): "Build-to-print drone Li-ion pack — AS9100 / ITAR-capable EMS partner sought"
  • For Tunisian / Eastern-EU low-cost targets (USI/AsteelFlash, ACTIA, Zollner HU/RO, Kitron LT/PL, Scanfil PL/EE): "EMS partner sought — drone battery pack assembly (Flyability)"

Routing line ("If you can route this to your business-development team for...")

Pick the segment label most likely to resonate inside that company's account-management structure:

Target profile Suggested segment label
Pure EMS, defense/aerospace strongAerospace & Defense
Pure EMS, energy/BESS strongEnergy & Cleantech
Automotive Tier-1 / battery-OEMIndustrial / e-Mobility
Generalist EMSIndustrial & Cleantech

Tier-based softening (volume-mismatched targets)

For Tier-3 / scale-mismatched players (Jabil, Flex, Ficosa, ICPT, Leoni, Webasto for very small MOQs) — append the following sentence before the sign-off to acknowledge the volume gap proactively:

"We recognise that 6–7 k packs/yr sits below your typical engagement threshold; we would still value a brief positioning call to understand whether a small-format program fits anywhere in your network or partner ecosystem."
