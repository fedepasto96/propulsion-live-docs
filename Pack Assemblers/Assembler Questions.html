Project: E4 Battery Project — Harmony Project
Prepared by: Federico Pastore (Lead Battery Engineer)

Purpose: Evaluate external battery pack assemblers for the E4 drone battery (pouch cells, ~6-7k packs/year)
Reference: Pack Assembler List in techno scouting spreadsheet; Outreach email drafts

Context: What We Need

PACK SUMMARY
We are sourcing a contract assembler for the E4 battery pack. The pack uses silicon-anode pouch cells (~350 Wh/kg), voltage range 2.7-4.3 V per cell, assembled into a custom pack with Flyability-designed BMS. Annual volume: ~6,000-7,000 packs/year at steady state. Application: industrial inspection drone operating in confined spaces (temperature extremes, vibration, dust).
KEY CONSTRAINT
US-assembled packs are strongly preferred for the US market (FCC authorization, NDAA compliance). EU-assembled packs are acceptable for non-US markets and as US spare batteries.

1. Technical Capability

Cell Handling & Assembly

CRITICAL 1.1 Do you have experience assembling packs from pouch cells? If so, what welding/bonding methods do you use for pouch cell tabs (ultrasonic, laser, resistance)?

Pouch cells require different tab welding than cylindrical. This is a hard filter.

CRITICAL 1.2 Can you work with customer-supplied cells (we ship cells to you for assembly), or do you only work with cells you source?

We are sourcing cells directly from the manufacturer (Amprius, EnPower, etc.).

CRITICAL 1.3 Can you integrate a customer-supplied BMS PCB into the pack? We provide the BMS design; you handle mechanical/electrical integration into the pack.

We own the BMS design. The assembler must not force their own BMS.

IMPORTANT 1.4 What cell formats and sizes have you worked with? Any experience with large-format pouch cells (>5 Ah)?

IMPORTANT 1.5 What is your typical pack assembly process? (e.g., cell sorting/matching, tab welding, BMS integration, potting/conformal coating, enclosure assembly, end-of-line testing)

We need to understand the full process flow and where our design inputs fit in.

1.6 Do you have in-house capability for thermal management integration (thermal pads, heat sinks, heating elements for cold operation)?

Testing & Validation

CRITICAL 1.7 What end-of-line tests do you perform on every pack? (OCV check, impedance, capacity grading, insulation resistance, functional BMS test, etc.)

IMPORTANT 1.8 Can you perform or coordinate UN 38.3 testing (transport certification)? In-house or via a partner lab?

1.9 Do you have environmental test chambers for temperature cycling, vibration, or altitude testing?

2. Quality & Certifications

CRITICAL 2.1 What quality certifications do you hold? (ISO 9001, AS9100, ITAR, IEC 62133, etc.)

IMPORTANT 2.2 Do you have full traceability from cell lot to finished pack? What system do you use? (serial number tracking, MES, etc.)

Traceability is required for safety recalls and field failure analysis on drones.

IMPORTANT 2.3 What is your defect rate / yield on production packs? What is your warranty policy for assembled packs?

2.4 Do you perform incoming cell inspection (OCV, impedance, visual) before assembly?

2.5 Can you share an example of your test report / certificate of conformity for a shipped pack?

Notes:

3. Supply Chain & Regulatory Compliance

CRITICAL 3.1 Where is your pack assembly physically located? (Country, state/region)

US assembly is required for FCC authorization of the drone. This is a hard filter for US-market packs.

CRITICAL 3.2 Is your supply chain NDAA-compliant? What percentage of your BOM (excluding customer-supplied cells and BMS) is non-FEOC?

US government end-users (DOD, DOE, etc.) require NDAA compliance.

IMPORTANT 3.3 Do you have experience with ITAR-controlled products or customers? (relevant for defense/government drone applications)

3.4 Can you handle hazmat shipping (UN 38.3 compliant packaging and labeling) for finished packs?

3.5 Do you source any passive components (connectors, wiring harnesses, enclosure parts) that we should be aware of from a country-of-origin perspective?

Notes:

4. Production & Scalability

CRITICAL 4.1 Can you support a volume of 6,000-7,000 packs/year (roughly 500-600/month)? What is your current available capacity?

IMPORTANT 4.2 What is your typical lead time from PO to first article? And from first article approval to steady-state production?

IMPORTANT 4.3 Describe your NPI (New Product Introduction) process. How many prototype/pilot builds do you typically do before production release?

4.4 What is your minimum order quantity (MOQ) per production run?

4.5 How do you handle engineering changes (ECOs) once production has started? What is the typical turnaround?

4.6 What level of automation is in your assembly line? (Manual, semi-auto, fully automated)

Affects consistency, yield, and scalability.

Notes:

5. Engineering Support & DFM

IMPORTANT 5.1 Do you offer DFM (Design for Manufacturability) review on customer pack designs? Can your engineers flag issues before we commit to tooling?

5.2 Can you support mechanical design of the pack enclosure, or do we need to provide a fully finished design? (e.g., do you have CAD/mechanical engineering in-house?)

5.3 Do you offer prototyping services (small batch, 5-20 units) before committing to production tooling?

5.4 What file formats and documentation do you need from us to start? (BOM, Gerber/ODB++ for BMS, 3D STEP, assembly drawings, test specs)

Notes:

6. Commercial

CRITICAL 6.1 What is your typical NRE (Non-Recurring Engineering) cost for a new pack program of this complexity? What does it cover?

IMPORTANT 6.2 Can you provide a rough unit cost estimate (assembly labor + materials, excluding cells and BMS) at 6,000 units/year? Even a ballpark range is useful.

6.3 What are your payment terms? (Net 30, milestone-based for NRE, etc.)

6.4 Do you require long-term volume commitments, or can we scale up/down year-over-year?

6.5 Is there a tooling cost for fixtures, jigs, or test equipment specific to our pack? Who owns the tooling?
